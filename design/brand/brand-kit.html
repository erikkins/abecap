Mark, palette, typography, and usage guidelines

Primary Mark

Bitone Spire

The primary mark is a bitone spire — claret for the upper lattice and compass arrow, ink for the lower cathedral body. Inspired by St. Peter's Church in Riga. No background circle, no container. The mark lives directly on paper or ink backgrounds.

On paper backgrounds

On ink backgrounds (cathedral body flips to paper)

The compass arrow at upper-right is intentional — it breaks the symmetry and creates directional energy. For circular social crops, offset the mark ~8% left to optically center it.

Wordmark

Logo + Type Lockup

The wordmark pairs the bitone spire with "RigaCap." set in Fraunces. The period is always claret. Never separate the mark from the text or rearrange them.

Typography

Type System

Three faces, three roles. Fraunces for headlines and symbols, IBM Plex Sans for body, IBM Plex Mono for data and prices.

Display — Fraunces

The market, measured.

Variable: opsz 9–144
Weights: 300–600
Use: Headlines, section titles, stock symbols

Body — IBM Plex Sans

Three-to-four signals a month, sometimes zero. We trade when the math is clear — not when the news is loud.

Weights: 300–600
Use: Body copy, descriptions, UI labels

Data — IBM Plex Mono

Weights: 400–500
Use: Prices, percentages, dates, code

Usage Guidelines

Do's and Don'ts

DO
RigaCap.
Always include the claret period. Mixed case: "RigaCap." never "RIGACAP" or "rigacap".
DON'T
RIGACAP
Don't use a different typeface, all-caps, or drop the period.
DO
On dark backgrounds, cathedral body switches to paper (#F5F1E8). Claret stays claret.
DON'T
Don't place on colored backgrounds. Only paper (#F5F1E8) or ink (#141210).
DO
Market, Measured.
Use Fraunces for all editorial headlines and branded phrases.
DON'T
Market, Measured.
Don't use non-brand typefaces or non-brand colors (no blue, no bright green, no neon).
Voice

Brand Voice

RigaCap speaks like a thoughtful colleague at dinner. Earnest, direct, grounded. Never like a fintech CEO on stage.

We sound like

  • “The system saw 3 names clear every gate this week.”
  • “Quiet weeks are normal. The math wasn't there.”
  • “We trade when the data is clear — not when the news is loud.”

We never sound like

  • “Our AI-powered algorithm detected a breakout signal!”
  • “Don't miss this incredible opportunity!”
  • “The tape is printing bullish price action on this name.”
